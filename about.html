About Northeast India's Green Wealth

Introduction

Northeast India is one of the richest biodiversity regions in the world. The states of Assam, Arunachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Manipur, Nagaland, Tripura, and Sikkim possess thousands of valuable horticultural and medicinal plant species. These plants support food security, traditional healthcare, sustainable agriculture, and economic development.

Importance of Horticultural Crops

The region is famous for its wide variety of fruits, vegetables, spices, plantation crops, flowers, and medicinal herbs. Favorable climate, fertile soils, and high rainfall make Northeast India an ideal place for cultivating oranges, pineapples, ginger, turmeric, large cardamom, black pepper, banana, and tea.

Importance of Medicinal Plants

Medicinal plants have been used by tribal communities for centuries. Many species possess antibacterial, antifungal, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and medicinal properties. These herbs are widely used in Ayurveda, herbal medicine, pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and essential oil industries.

Conclusion

The horticultural and medicinal plants of Northeast India represent an important natural resource. Conserving this biodiversity while promoting scientific cultivation can improve farmers' income, strengthen healthcare, and protect the environment for future generations.
